Deep Dive: The Logic of Divergence

1. Belly Capacity Effect
Grounded passenger flights led to a sharp drop in global capacity, driving up cargo yields. The cargo upturn is partly due to premiums from "supply shortages."
2. Price-Volume Decoupling
Airlines adopted a "Protect Price, Control Volume" strategy. While passenger counts haven't peaked, high fares enable a "High-Yield Recovery."
3. Cargo Structural Shift
Growth drivers shifted from 2020's "Medical Supplies" to 2025's "Cross-border E-commerce." This provides long-term structural support.

Executive Summary

  • Surface Trend: A typical K-shaped divergence where cargo rises while passenger traffic struggles.
  • Core Logic: A microcosm of global supply chain restructuring (Strong Cargo) vs. restricted cross-border mobility (Weak Passenger).
  • Future Outlook: As passenger capacity returns, cargo yields will normalize, but E-commerce remains the new cornerstone of volume.
